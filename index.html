Frequently Asked Questions

Get answers to help guide you when a pet goes missing.

I lost my pet. Who should I call? keyboard_arrow_down

First, upload a picture of your pet to begin your search on Petco Love Lost. We use facial recognition technology to help match lost pets nationwide. Next, call your local animal shelter or animal control agency. Give them your contact information, with a backup contact to ensure that you can respond to any sightings promptly. Also conduct a search of your nearby area. Most pets are found within a few blocks of where they went missing.

Can I report a lost dog? keyboard_arrow_down

Yes. With Petco Love Lost, you can post a picture of the lost dog on our database. Share the pet listing you created to a social network like Nextdoor or Facebook. Call your local animal shelter, non-emergency police number or animal control office. Alerting these channels can let them know to be on the lookout for a lost pet.

Should I offer a reward for my lost dog? keyboard_arrow_down

In short, no. Offering a reward may result in many more people chasing and trying to capture the lost dog, which can make recovery more difficult. Instead of offering a reward, list “Please Help: Lost Dog" on your flyer, poster or social media post.

How long can a lost pet survive? keyboard_arrow_down

Cats have excellent survival instincts. Some have been found alive weeks after they went missing, even when trapped without food or water. It's not uncommon for pets to be found months — or years, in some rare cases — after their family has reported them lost.

What type of information should I include on a lost pet poster? keyboard_arrow_down

The most effective posters are big and brightly-colored, with large and easy-to-read wording. Print the most relevant information in dark, bold lettering. Include the following information in your poster: The pet’s name When and where it was last seen A physical description of the animal Any additional information, such as “do not chase” if the pet is shy Your phone number For an easy flyer template you can print at home, upload your pet to Petco Love Lost and visit your dashboard and print your pet listing.

What should I do if my lost pet returns home? keyboard_arrow_down

Make sure that your lost pet is physically healthy when they return home. Be aware that going to the veterinarian can be stressful for an already stressed-out pet, so give them a little time to acclimate before heading in for a checkup unless they need medical attention immediately. As your pet settles back in, try to reduce the number of visitors in the initial days upon return. If you created a lost pet listing on Petco Love Lost, click on My Dashboard, click into your pet’s details and click “Mark as Safe.”
